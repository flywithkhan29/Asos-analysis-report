# Asos-analysis-report
I've analyzed your ASOS product analysis code and created a comprehensive Data Analysis Report


 Problem Statement
Core Business Question:

"Which product brands are losing the most revenue due to inventory stockouts, and what is the relationship between pricing strategy and inventory availability?"

Secondary Questions:

How significant is the "phantom revenue" loss (revenue from out-of-stock sizes)?
Do premium brands experience different stockout patterns than budget brands?
Which brands should prioritize inventory replenishment?


Recommendations
For Inventory Management

Priority 1: Focus on brands in the RED ZONE (Premium + High Stockout)
Priority 2: Implement demand forecasting improvements for budget brands with high stockout rates
Priority 3: Benchmark green zone brands' supply chain practices

For Product Strategy

Monitor stockout rates alongside pricing to ensure price value proposition
Consider dynamic inventory levels based on brand positioning
Set SKU-level stockout targets aligned with brand strategy

For Revenue Optimization

Recover "phantom revenue" through:

Inventory optimization
Backorder management
Strategic restock prioritization


Conclusion
This analysis transforms raw inventory data into strategic insights about revenue leakage from stockouts. By identifying which brands lose the most revenue due to inventory issues and visualizing their market positioning, stakeholders can prioritize inventory investments and improve overall supply chain efficiency.
Bottom Line: The scatter plot creates a clear visual hierarchy for action—brands in the upper-right quadrant (high price, high stockout) should receive immediate attention as they represent premium customers with unmet demand.
